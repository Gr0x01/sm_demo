## Tier 1: Must-Have Software Review Sites

These are where builders and their ops teams actually research software. Higharc (your closest comp) is listed on G2 with 23 badges. You need to be here.

### 1. G2
- **URL**: https://www.g2.com / https://sell.g2.com/create-a-profile
- **Cost**: Free basic profile. Paid plans unlock analytics, lead gen, enhanced branding.
- **Why it matters**: 100M+ software buyers. Higharc has 23 G2 Spring 2025 badges across 5 categories. G2 Grid reports are referenced by decision-makers. Being absent here while Higharc is ranked is a competitive gap.
- **Categories to target**: "Home Builder Software", "Construction Management", potentially request a new category for "Design Center Software" or "Upgrade Visualization"
- **Gotchas**: Profile approval takes 3-5 business days. Minimum 10 reviews to appear on G2 Grid reports. Reviews must come from verified users (G2 verifies via LinkedIn). Without reviews you exist but are invisible in rankings.
- **Action**: Claim free profile immediately. Populate with screenshots, demo video, product description. Reviews come later (see timing section).

### 2. Capterra (+ GetApp + Software Advice)
- **URL**: https://www.capterra.com/vendors/sign-up
- **Cost**: Free basic listing. Paid = PPC bid for "Visit Website" button placement.
- **Why it matters**: Capterra has a dedicated "Home Builder Software" category (https://www.capterra.com/home-builder-software/). This is exactly where your ICP searches. GetApp and Software Advice are part of the same Gartner Digital Markets network -- one signup covers all three.
- **Categories to target**: "Home Builder Software", "Construction Management Software", "Construction CRM Software"
- **Gotchas**: Free listing gets you in the directory but you're below paid listings. No minimum reviews to appear, but reviews improve ranking. Gartner acquired these from Capterra in Feb 2026 -- expect changes.
- **Action**: Sign up at capterra.com/vendors/sign-up. One submission covers Capterra, GetApp, and Software Advice. High priority because the "Home Builder Software" category is a perfect fit.

### 3. TrustRadius
- **URL**: https://www.trustradius.com/vendor / https://solutions.trustradius.com/claim-your-profile/
- **Cost**: Free basic profile. Paid plans for enhanced features.
- **Why it matters**: Enterprise-leaning review site. Builders doing due diligence often land here. Long-form, verified reviews carry more weight than star ratings. TrustRadius reviews are syndicated to other platforms.
- **Gotchas**: Request access at vendor.trustradius.com, reviewed by TrustRadius team. Reviews are detailed (2,000+ character minimum) so harder to collect but more credible. No minimum to appear, but empty profiles look bad.
- **Action**: Request access after you have at least 1-2 pilot customers who can write substantial reviews. Not urgent for pre-revenue.

### 4. SoftwareConnect

## Getting Early Reviews: The Playbook

The chicken-and-egg problem: directories reward reviews, but you need customers to get reviews.

### Strategy 1: Pilot Customers Are Review Goldmines
Every pilot contract should include a soft ask: "If this works, would you share your experience on G2/Capterra?" Not in the contract -- in the relationship. Design center managers are especially willing because the tool makes their life easier.

### Strategy 2: Two Reviews Per Pilot
Target two reviews per pilot customer: one from the decision-maker (VP/owner -- speaks to ROI) and one from the design center manager (daily user -- speaks to usability). G2 and Capterra both benefit from role diversity in reviews.

### Strategy 3: Make It Painless
Send the exact link. Write a suggested outline ("What problem were you solving? How does Finch compare to your old process? What results have you seen?"). Offer to do it over the phone and transcribe. The fewer barriers, the more reviews you get.

### Strategy 4: G2 Review Generation Campaigns
G2 has a built-in review generation tool (even on free plans). You can send review request emails through their platform. Incentives are allowed (gift cards up to $25) as long as you don't influence the content.

### Strategy 5: Time It Right
Ask for reviews 30-60 days into a pilot -- after the "wow" moment but before the novelty fades. This is when design center managers are most enthusiastic and have concrete results to cite.

### Strategy 6: Capterra Reviews for Visibility
Capterra weights recency and volume. Even 3-5 reviews in the first quarter of a listing puts you above most competitors in the "Home Builder Software" category (many listed products have <10 reviews).
