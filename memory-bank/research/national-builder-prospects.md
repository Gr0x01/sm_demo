## TIER 1

### 1. Dream Finders Homes
- **HQ**: Jacksonville, FL | ~9,250 homes/yr | Public (DFH)
- **Tech Level**: Low
- **Tech Notes**: Physical design studio with in-person appointments. Design consultants walk buyers through brand-name selections (flooring, countertops, cabinets, appliances). No online configurator or visualization tool found. Standard sample-board-and-PDF approach. Large company but tech-conservative on the design center side.
- **Contact 1**: David Zalupski — VP of Sales and Marketing
- **Contact 2**: Doug Moran — National President (formerly SVP/COO, oversees all operations)
- **LinkedIn Search**: `"Dream Finders Homes" VP Sales OR "division president" OR "design center"`
- **Notes**: Public company, 4 segments (Southeast, Mid-Atlantic, Midwest, Financial Services). Multiple division presidents across regions. High volume = high upgrade revenue opportunity. Conservative but revenue-driven.

### 2. David Weekley Homes
- **HQ**: Houston, TX | ~5,500 homes/yr | Private
- **Tech Level**: Low-Medium
- **Tech Notes**: MyDWHome.com buyer portal with lifestyle questionnaire, wish list builder, and product research (photos, videos, use/care guides). INSPIRATIONS curated design packages. Still fundamentally an in-person design center experience — the online tools are prep/research, not visualization. No AI or interactive configurator. Multiple design centers across TX, NC, FL, CO.
- **Contact 1**: Sonya Mills — VP of Marketing and Communications
- **Contact 2**: Josh Hilscher — Director of Sales (formerly VP Sales & Marketing)
- **LinkedIn Search**: `"David Weekley Homes" VP Sales OR "design center" OR "design consultant director"`
- **Notes**: Largest private builder in America. Known for quality and culture. The MyDWHome portal shows tech willingness but it's a prep tool, not a selling tool. Strong design center infrastructure = good fit for Finch overlay.

Most digitally advanced of this group but focused on floor plans/architecture, not upgrade/finish visualization.
- **Contact 1**: Tony Albachiara — SVP of Sales
- **Contact 2**: Carrie Schonberg — Chief Marketing Officer (joined 2012 as first VP Marketing, ex-Kodak digital)
- **Alt Contact**: Angela Roby Hogan — Director of Design (runs The Studio)
- **LinkedIn Search**: `"Ashton Woods" SVP Sales OR CMO OR "design studio" OR "Director of Design"`
- **Notes**: Best brand positioning of the group. Carrie Schonberg has deep digital/e-commerce background (Kodak, Walmart, Amazon partnerships). Angela Roby Hogan as Director of Design is the internal champion target. They already invest in visualization (Zonda VR) but it's floor-plan focused, not upgrade/finish focused. The gap between their floor plan visualization and their finish selection process is exactly where Finch lives. Regional presidents: Lindsay Motley (TX), Dan Fitzpatrick (FL/TN).

### 5. Drees Homes
- **HQ**: Fort Mitchell, KY | ~3,000 homes/yr | Private
- **Tech Level**: Medium-High
- **Tech Notes**: Uses Envision Options platform (edc2.envisionoptions.com) — a real online design configurator where buyers can explore options, finishes, upgrades, and pricing by floor plan. Also has interactive floor plan tool. Multiple physical design centers. This is the most tech-forward builder on the list. Already uses a competitor product (Envision/BDX ecosystem).
- **Contact 1**: Barbara Jones — VP of Marketing
- **Contact 2**: Marla Telfair — Corporate Director of Sales
- **Alt Contact**: Karen Wiesner — National Marketing Director
- **LinkedIn Search**: `"Drees Homes" VP Sales OR "design center" OR "Director of Sales" OR marketing`
- **Notes**: Already on Envision = they've validated the category. The question is whether Envision gives them visualization or just configuration. Envision is a configurator, not a visualizer — buyers pick options from dropdowns but don't see AI-generated images. Finch's pitch: "You already invested in online selection. Now show them what it looks like." David Drees is CEO (family company). Dan Jones is EVP of Homebuilding Operations.

## PRIORITY RANKING (Finch fit)

### Best Fits (high volume + low tech = biggest gap to fill)

1. **Dream Finders Homes** — 9,250 homes/yr, Low tech, public company with margin pressure. Massive upgrade revenue opportunity.
2. **Eastwood Homes** — 1,800 homes/yr, already investing in visualization for structural. Natural extension to finishes. Bryan DeGabrielle is a good target.
3. **Homes By WestBay** — 1,200 homes/yr, invested in design studio experience but no visualization. Dee Crescini (VP Design) is ideal entry point.
4. **David Weekley Homes** — 5,500 homes/yr, has online tools but no visualization. Massive volume.

### Good Fits (tech-interested, need the right angle)

5. **Ashton Woods** — Already uses Zonda VR for floor plans. Pitch the gap: "You visualize the house but not the finishes." Carrie Schonberg gets digital.
6. **Kolter Homes** — Heavy investment in physical design studios. Active adult buyers need MORE help visualizing. Marc Friedman (ex-hospitality) thinks about experience.
7. **GHO Homes** — Small but fast decision-making. Good pilot candidate. Nancy Villafane is the internal champion.
8. **ICI Homes** — Very traditional but Mori Hosseini's NAHB connections are strategically valuable.

### Harder Sells

9. **Drees Homes** — Already on Envision. Pitch is "visualization on top of your existing configurator" but they may feel locked in.
10. **Stanley Martin** — Japanese corporate parent adds decision-making complexity. Multiple division presidents to convince.
